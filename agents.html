// how the teams work together

The real lift is the hand-off.

Not one agent — your teams passing work across your whole operation, automatically.

Workflow 01

Missed call → booked job → confirmed crew

A homeowner calls after hours. They're booked, the crew is notified, and they have a text reminder by 8am. Front Office → Back Office → Marketing.

Workflow 02

Web lead → instant quote → won deal

An 11pm inquiry gets an itemized estimate against your pricing book, with follow-up at day 2, 5, and 10. Sales → Back Office → Marketing.

Workflow 03

Overdue customer → reactivation → renewal

240 customers overdue 6+ months each get a message tied to their last visit. Polite, persistent. They book. Marketing → Front Office → Sales.

Workflow 04

Morning chaos → six-line brief → calm

Inbox, calendar, activity log, open work — read overnight. By 7am you have a six-line briefing: what happened, what needs you, what's at risk. Back Office → Daily brief.

Workflow 05

Stalled handoff → caught → fixed

A file sits idle 8 hours past SLA. It pings the right human, drafts the apology, and logs the leak so Friday-at-5pm doesn't repeat. Back Office → Exception log.

Workflow 06

AR backlog → cleared invoices → books closed

28 invoices past 7 days get the right tone of follow-up; the worst three escalate for a personal touch. Books close by the 3rd. Finance → Sales → Close.
