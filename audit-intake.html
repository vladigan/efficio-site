Day 1 onboarding — what we collect

Total customer effort: ~25 minutes.

Most agencies make you maintain spreadsheets, write reports, and feed them data forever. We don't. You connect a few tools, drop a few documents, answer a short intake form, and we take it from there. Everything below is exactly what we ask for and why.

1

Tools you'll connect (one-click OAuth)

~10 min

All connections are read-only. We never write to your systems and never get your passwords. You revoke access any time from inside each tool's settings page.

RequiredStripePayment processor connection — read revenue, customers, churn.
RequiredPlaid (bank)Read-only bank feed for cashflow + true revenue picture.
RequiredQuickBooks OnlineOr Xero / FreshBooks — accounting source of truth.
RequiredYour CRMHubSpot / Pipedrive / GoHighLevel / ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro / Jobber — pick one.
RequiredGoogle WorkspaceRead-only calendar + Gmail metadata — never message content.
If applicableMeta + Google AdsSpend, ROAS, conversion attribution — if you're running paid traffic.
2

Documents to drop in your private Dropbox folder

~5 min

We create a private Dropbox folder for you on the audit call. Drop these into it whenever it's convenient before kickoff:

  • Last 3 months of bank statementsSo Hermes can map your real cashflow patterns and seasonality.
  • Customer roster (CSV export from your CRM)Names, emails, last-touch dates, lifetime value — whatever you have.
  • 5–10 recent invoices (PDF or screenshot)Anonymize them if you want — we just need to see the layout, line items, terms.
  • Sales / service reports (last 30 days)Whatever you already export weekly — daily appointments, win/loss, route logs. Skip it if you don't have one.
3

10 quick questions

~10 min

Sent as a form right after the audit call so you can fill it on your time. Here's what we ask, so nothing surprises you:

Q1Owner name + role — who's the decision-maker on this and what's your day-to-day?
Q2Team size + roles — how many people, what do they own?
Q3Top 3 workflows eating your time right now — owner's words, not industry jargon.
Q4How are leads coming in today? — phone, web form, walk-in, referral, paid ads.
Q5What happens to a missed call right now? — voicemail, callback queue, lost lead.
Q6How are quotes/estimates produced today? — by whom, in what tool, how long.
Q7What's the owner doing in the inbox that another role should be doing?
Q8What's the one metric you'd most want to see move in 30 days?
Q9What have you already tried for the same problem? AI tools, VAs, hires, software, etc.
Q10Anything off-limits — accounts we shouldn't touch, customers we shouldn't contact, hours we shouldn't ping?
4

Security — the part most agencies skip

We treat your data like the IRS is auditing us tomorrow, because the businesses we serve can't afford to find out otherwise.

Access level
Read-only · always
Passwords
Never stored or seen
Revocable
By you · any time
Where data lives
Your cockpit · not shared
  • OAuth, not credentials.Every tool connection uses the platform's own OAuth flow — we never see passwords, never store tokens longer than needed, and you click "Revoke" inside Stripe / QuickBooks / Google whenever you want us out.
  • Your cockpit, your data.Each customer gets their own Hermes instance with their own memory file and storage. Your business data is never blended with another customer's or sold to anyone.
  • Reversible at any time.Cancel the service, revoke OAuth, request a data wipe — and within 7 days everything we have on you is gone. Confirmation email when it's done.
