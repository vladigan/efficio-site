Case Study 01 · Job Search Automation

3,186 LinkedIn Applications.
34% Response Rate.

A single AI pipeline replaced 200+ hours of manual job-search clicking — and outperformed the industry baseline by 3x.

The Pain

Job searching at volume is mechanical: find a posting, copy-paste your info, write a tailored cover message, click submit, track it somewhere. Do that 50 times a day and you've spent 3–4 hours on work that has zero compounding value. The typical job seeker applies to 10–20 roles before burnout sets in. The typical response rate on manual applications hovers under 10%. The gap between "what the market expects" and "what a human can actually sustain" is the problem this system solves.

The Build

  • LinkedIn scraper + scorer: pulls active Easy Apply postings matching a target role profile, runs each listing through a fit-score model (title match, seniority band, location, company size, remote flag) — applies only to listings above threshold. No spray-and-pray.
  • AI-personalized cover messages: Claude generates a role-specific opening paragraph per application using the job description + a fixed personal profile. Every message reads individually written. No template bleed.
  • Application executor: Playwright-based browser automation handles the Easy Apply form flow, including multi-step applications, resume uploads, and the “additional question” fields that trip up naive bots.
  • Tracking + reply classifier: all sent applications log to a local SQLite DB with timestamp, company, role, and status. Inbound replies are classified automatically (interview request / rejection / info request / ghost) so nothing falls through.

The Takeaway

The 34% response rate isn't a product of volume alone — it's because the fit scorer filters out noise before the application fires. Indiscriminate volume actually hurts response rate (ATS systems flag spammy applicants). The right AI build is specific: score first, personalize second, execute third. That sequence applies equally to job search, client outreach, and federal-contract bids. The underlying pattern is the same pipeline, different data.
