vs. GoHighLevel

The platform vs. the team

GoHighLevel is a DIY automation and CRM platform. Everything you'd build in Efficio you can build in GHL — if you also become the implementer, the operator, the maintainer, and the help desk. A lot of agencies resell it. A lot of agencies also disappear after handing you the login.

Where GHL wins

  • Cheapest entry point ($297/mo)
  • Best fit if you're an agency selling to clients
  • Massive feature surface (calendars, funnels, CRM, automations)
  • Big community + tutorials

Where it breaks for owners

  • You're the implementer (200+ hours to set up well)
  • You're the operator (your staff has to run it daily)
  • You're the maintainer (workflows break, you fix)
  • No accountability when it goes silent at 2pm
  • Reseller "white-label" agencies often vanish after onboarding
Pick GHL if

You're an agency reselling to your own clients, OR you have a dedicated ops person who genuinely wants to spend 10–15 hrs/week running the platform. If neither applies, you're going to either (a) pay $297/mo to use 6% of it or (b) hire a GHL consultant for $4K/mo and now you're at our price anyway.

vs. Hiring in-house

Four humans, three months, fingers crossed

The "real" version of the AI team you'd want: one receptionist ($30–50K/yr), one office manager ($45–90K), one sales rep ($50–100K), one bookkeeper ($40–80K). Plus benefits, taxes, training, turnover, and a manager to coordinate them. This is what we replace, role-for-role.

Where in-house wins

  • Real humans with real judgment
  • They can do work AI legitimately can't (yet)
  • Cultural fit if you have a team-building advantage
  • Right call at $5M+ where workload is stable

Where it breaks below $5M/mo

  • $165K–$320K/yr in salaries before benefits + taxes
  • 3–6 months to recruit + ramp each role
  • One quits and you're back to drowning
  • You're now a manager of 4 humans, not running a business
  • 40% of your week is interviewing + reviewing + firing
Pick in-house if

You're at $5M+ MRR with stable demand and the budget to keep all 4 roles full year-round. Below that, the human cost is irrational vs. what we deliver for $497–$3,500/mo. We literally do the same work the same way you'd want a human to.

vs. an AI consultancy

Six months, three decks, one engineer who left

Deloitte, BCG, Accenture, and the boutique AI shops they spun out of. They quote a 90-day discovery, charge $15K–$50K/mo, ship a strategy document, hand you a pilot, then leave you to operationalize it. We've watched this pattern destroy more SMB AI budgets than any other path.

Where consultancies win

  • Enterprise digital transformation ($50M+ budgets)
  • Cross-functional strategy work (real consulting)
  • Brand credibility if your board needs a Big 4 logo
  • Deep specialization if you have unusual data needs

Where it breaks for SMBs

  • $15K–$50K/mo retainers (10–100x ours)
  • Discovery phase delivers a deck, not a deployed agent
  • "Implementation phase" means more billable hours
  • They're optimized for partner billing rate, not your ROI
  • Junior engineer doing the actual work has 5 other clients
Pick a consultancy if

You're a Fortune 500 doing a regulated, multi-year, cross-business-unit AI rollout that needs change management, exec alignment, and procurement-grade documentation. We refer those to QueryNow. If you're below $50M revenue, a consultancy will quietly eat 18 months and $300K. We deliver a working agent in 7 days.
