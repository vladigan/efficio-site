// path 1 · DIY platform

The platform vs. the team.

Everything you'd build with us, you can build yourself on a DIY platform — if you also become the implementer, the operator, the maintainer, and the help desk.

Where DIY breaks for owners

  • You're the implementer — 200+ hours to set up well
  • You're the operator — staff runs it daily
  • You're the maintainer — workflows break, you fix
  • No accountability when it goes silent at 2pm
  • "White-label" resellers often vanish after onboarding

Pick DIY only if

  • You're an agency reselling to your own clients
  • You have a dedicated ops person who wants to run it 10–15 hrs/week
  • Otherwise you pay to use 6% of it — or hire a consultant and land at our price anyway
// path 2 · hire a team of humans

Four humans, three months, fingers crossed.

The "real" version of the AI team you'd want: a receptionist, an office manager, a sales rep, a bookkeeper — plus benefits, taxes, training, turnover, and a manager to coordinate them. This is what we replace, role-for-role.

Where headcount breaks below $5M/mo

  • $165K–$320K/yr in salaries before benefits + taxes
  • 3–6 months to recruit + ramp each role
  • One quits and you're back to drowning
  • You become a manager of 4 humans, not a business owner
  • 40% of your week: interviewing, reviewing, firing

Pick hiring only if

  • You're at $5M+ with stable, year-round demand
  • You have budget to keep all four roles full
  • Below that, the human cost is irrational vs. $997–$3,500/mo for the same work
// path 3 · a consulting project

Six months, three decks, one engineer who left.

A long discovery, a fat retainer, a strategy document, a pilot — then you're left to operationalize it. This pattern has destroyed more SMB AI budgets than any other.

Where a project breaks for SMBs

  • $15K–$50K/mo retainers — 10–100x ours
  • Discovery delivers a deck, not a deployed agent
  • "Implementation phase" means more billable hours
  • Optimized for partner billing rate, not your ROI
  • The junior doing the work has five other clients

Pick a project only if

  • You're enterprise, doing a regulated, multi-year, cross-unit rollout
  • You need change management, exec alignment, procurement-grade docs
  • Below that, a project quietly eats 18 months and $300K — we ship a working agent in 7 days
