The things that quietly cost you patients

Owner-to-owner. You already know these. The question is who's fixing them while you're with a patient.

The front desk can't be in two places at once

One person can't answer every call and book every appointment at the same time. When the phone rings during a busy hour, it goes to voicemail — and a lot of those callers don't call back. They book somewhere else.

Empty chairs from no-shows and last-minute cancellations

A late cancellation leaves a chair or a room sitting empty for an hour you'll never bill. Nobody had time to work the waitlist and fill it, so the slot just evaporates.

Insurance gets collected too late

When coverage details aren't gathered before the visit, the patient stalls at check-in while someone scrambles to verify it — and the whole morning runs behind.

Intake is a paperwork wall

New patients hit a stack of forms before anyone's even greeted them. It's slow, it's a bad first impression, and it eats front-desk time you don't have.

What the AI team handles

A Front Office team that owns the whole function

Not one bot you have to babysit — a coordinated team of agents that runs the front desk together, and a Back Office team that keeps the schedule full. Want the full breakdown of how a team is built? See how it works.

Booking & rescheduling, around the clock

The Front Office team answers the phone and your online booking day and night, finds the right slot, books it, and reschedules without a single ring going to voicemail. Patients book when it's convenient for them, not just during desk hours.

No-show & cancellation follow-up that re-books the slot

When someone cancels or doesn't show, the Back Office team follows up right away and works the waitlist to fill the opening — so the chair doesn't sit empty.

Insurance details collected & verified before the visit

The team gathers and confirms coverage up front, so check-in is clean and the visit isn't stalling at the desk over a card nobody asked for in advance.

New-patient intake captured before they arrive

Intake is collected and organized ahead of time, so the first thing a new patient feels is a smooth welcome — not a clipboard. Your staff gets a ready-to-go chart instead of a paperwork backlog.

Recare & recall outreach that brings patients back on schedule

The Back Office team runs your recall list — cleanings, wellness exams, annual checks, plan follow-ups — and reaches out at the right time to get patients back in the book. This is the work that's always "we'll get to it," and it's exactly the work that keeps the schedule full. Going deeper by vertical: see the dental practice page and the medical clinic page.
